## 6.3 log functions

$$let f(x)=b^x for  b>1$$
$$then f'(x)=b^x*f'(0)>0$$

hence f is increasing from $(-\infin,\infin)$

$\log_b(x)$ is inverse function to $b^x$

$\log_bx=y$ if $b^y=x$

### log rules

#### addition

$$\log_bxy=\log_bx+\log_by$$

#### subtraction

$$\log_bx/y=\log_bx-\log_by$$

#### natural log

$$\log_ex = \ln x$$
$$e^{\ln x}=x for x>0$$
$$\ln e^x, for all x$$
